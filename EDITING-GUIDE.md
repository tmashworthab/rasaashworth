# How to edit Rasa Ashworth's website

A quick guide to changing the words and pictures on **rasaashworth.com**.
You don't need to know any code — it's all point-and-click.

---

## 1. Logging in

1. Open **https://rasaashworth.sanity.studio**
2. Sign in with the account you were invited with (Google, or the email the
   invite was sent to).

That's your editor. It's private — only you can change anything; visitors to
the website never see it.

---

## 2. What you can edit

When you log in you'll see two things in the left-hand list:

**🖼  Artworks** — every piece in the gallery.
Click a piece to change its:
- **Title** — what the piece is called
- **Series** — the little label (Still Life, Figures, Landscape, Seascapes)
- **Description** — the text shown when someone clicks the piece
- **Image** — the photo itself
- **Display order** — lower numbers show first in the gallery

**📝  Site text** — the words on the rest of the site, all on one screen:
- The **home page** tagline and quote
- The **About** biography
- The **contact** email, studio location and the British Tapestry Group link

---

## 3. Making a change

1. Click the thing you want to edit (e.g. an artwork, or "Site text").
2. Click into a box and type your change.
3. Click the **Publish** button (bottom of the screen).

✅ **That's it.** Your change appears on the live website,
**www.rasaashworth.com**, within about a minute. (Refresh the page if you don't
see it straight away.)

---

## 4. A few handy tips

- **Changing a photo:** open the artwork, click the image, choose **Replace**,
  and upload the new one. Then **Publish**.
- **Reordering the gallery:** give each artwork a "Display order" number
  (1, 2, 3…) — smaller numbers appear first.
- **Made a mistake?** Just edit it again and Publish. Nothing is permanent, and
  you can't break the website by typing — at worst a change just doesn't show.
- **"Draft" vs "Published":** as you type it saves a draft automatically. Your
  change only goes live when you press **Publish**.
